abstract: "The researchers examined how microplastics accumulate in three gastropod species with different feeding patterns collected from Balekambang Beach, Java. Microplastics were found in all specimens, dominated by fragments (94.51%), followed by fibres (4.68%), with particles primarily sized 20–40 µm and predominantly black. Analysis identified polystyrene and nylon polymers. Microplastic abundance exhibited a clear gradient across feeding guilds: highest in detritivores (148.21 particles/g), intermediate in carnivores (143.82 particles/g), and lowest in herbivores (56.19 particles/g), demonstrating that feeding ecology significantly shapes bioaccumulation patterns in these marine organisms."
